Meow Cat Rescue

Jan 2019 | 6 Weeks

Role | User research, Interface Design

Meow Cat Rescue is a non-profit cat shelter. Their website has been used for more than 10 years as a significant channel to inform and engage their visitors. Meow Cat Rescue is not happy with their existing website because it is “confusing and appears to be missing vital information”. They need a new website to improve their chances of bringing in more qualified visitors who are more likely to make an adoption. My challenge is to create a new website that would be intuitive, fresh and lovely.

I address with the users about the current website, here are some INSIGHTS:

  1. There is too much text on every webpage and was overwhelming for the audience to find the content they want.
  2. It was not clear how to help, including how to donate and how to volunteer.
  3. The shelter’s contact information was missing from the home page.

To explore the reasons for why is the old website underperforming, I analyzed the web analytics data. My analysis revealed that new visitors were able to find the website but they were not staying long. The website had a high bounce rate and low engagement metrics.

Traffic sources, search keywords, and landing page reports provided insight into the kind of information visitors were looking for and helped to identify the website's content gaps.

In addition, Mobile reports showed that more and more users view the site using a mobile device. Being that the site was not responsive and did not render correctly on small screens, this was a significant usability problem.

For me, it is essential for the new website to deliver a strong motivation and a positive attitude to its visitors. I created a mood board to set the visual direction for this project to get an early sign-off. The visual elements are created to aim to spark an emotional response from visitors.

Wireframes are like architectural blueprints. I created low-fidelity wireframes to map out the new website's structure and page layouts. These were great for communicating the design vision and getting early feedback. The wireframes went through a couple rounds of iteration before going further to next step.

User testing should be conducted continually throughout the creation process. In this case I wanted to make sure I was on the right track before completing my high-fidelity mockups. My approach was to create a mock-up of the homepage first and then test it with recruited users.. I showed participants my design and asked them: “How does this page make you feel? Please list your top three emotions."

Here is the result I got. As you can see, most feedback is positive and captured the desired mood perfectly. However, I still got some negative responses as “badly-organized”, “distracted”. Based on this, I modified the design by reducing the amount of colour, adding titles to clearly define every part of the content.
