My Journay Into UX

I started to learn painting when I was a kid. It was a great podium to progress and discover my creativity. This lifetime hobby makes me especially sensitive to color, pattern, and design.
I have a degree in Architetural Design. Architects think in terms of building, space, details, and people expeirence. These big-picture skills, along with all the technical skills prepare me to excel in UX design.
I enjoy design and being creative. I worked as a freelance visual designer when I am pursing my Ph.D. degree in Urban Planning. I helped several small business to identify their LOGO and print.
Most architecture schools have at least one or two courses on architecture and human behavior to develop skills for incorporating behavioral factors in the design process. UCD is also the guiding principle for UX Design.
I have worked in a research lab focused on Architectural Design and Human Behavior. This experience gives me lots of expereince on data analysis, which helps clients define and measure key performance metrics and usability.
I transited into UX design because I recognize the need for both qualitative and quantitative research. I also love having an active role in designing a better solution. I believe that understanding your users and acknowledging their needs and frustrations is the key to improving functionality, ease of use and enjoyment.

My Design Process

User-centered design is the soul of all design process. UCD requires designers to not only analyze and foresee how users are likely to use a product, but also to test the validity of their assumptions with regard to routine user behavior in the real world. Users deeply influence how a design takes shape.

UX design is a non-linear, iterative process. This involves consistent process management for usefully timed user-producer communication in a cyclical design evolution process. Good communication with users and the collaboration with different teams are important to achieve an engaging and enjoyable user experience.
